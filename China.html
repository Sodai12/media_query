Guilin · China
The Longji Terrace nestled in the Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region of China. As autumn approaches, the fields begin to ripen, and in the morning and evening, the sun would casts a radiant golden hue over the landscape. During my gap year, I had the opportunity to live in Longji with my family for a short stay. Longji is home to several ethnic minority groups of China, and living amidst them gave a unique insights into their distinct culinary practices and traditions
Chengdu · China
Chengdu, my hometown, is often synonymous with two things: its world-famous Sichuan cuisine and the adorable giant pandas. But beyond these iconic associations, there's a pace and style of living in Chengdu that truly sets it apart. In stark contrast to the rapid hustle and bustle of cities like New York, Chengdu thrives on a leisurely rhythm. Here, it's not unusual for people to take a break from their workday to visit a local teahouse, sipping on freshly brewed tea and perhaps engaging in a game of mahjong or simply indulging in some laid-back conversation. Summers in Chengdu can be oppressively hot and humid, but escape is just a short drive away. The surrounding mountains not only offer a refreshing retreat from the heat but are also home to numerous Daoist temples – serene sanctuaries where spiritual seekers have come to practice and meditate for centuries.
